The scenario

A growing brand with no single source of truth

Norah & Co is modeled as a direct-to-consumer skincare brand selling across the US, UK, and Canada through Shopify, with paid acquisition running on Meta and Google. The scenario is a common one: a product-first founder who is excellent at the brand and the formulation, but reviews performance by manually cross-checking Shopify's dashboard, the ad platforms, and a spreadsheet that had outgrown anyone's trust in it. Revenue was climbing, but nobody could say with confidence whether the unit economics underneath it were improving or quietly getting worse.

What the data was hiding

Four problems the dashboard was built to surface

Acquisition cost, rising unnoticed

Meta CAC moved from $18.07 in January to $40.83 in December — a 126% increase across the year — while headline revenue kept climbing, masking the trend.

No combined view

Orders, ad spend, and reach lived in three disconnected systems. Calculating a true blended ROAS took an hour of manual work, and the result wasn't fully trusted even then.

An invisible retention gap

Repeat purchase rate sat at 19.1%, well under the 28.5% DTC benchmark — a gap nobody was tracking against a reference point, so it read as normal.

Concentration risk in one SKU

The Vitamin C Serum alone drove 32.4% of revenue, with two SKUs contributing under 5% combined — a dependency nobody had quantified.

The build

Three layers, from raw data to a live view

Python pipeline

A documented script ingests orders, customers, products, and ad spend, validates the schema, and outputs nine structured reports covering every KPI the business needs — built to run on a schedule via cron or a GitHub Action.

Excel executive dashboard

A five-tab workbook where every visible number traces to a formula against the raw data — never a hardcoded figure. Updating it for a new month means pasting new rows into the source tabs.

Power BI model

A full semantic model with 20-plus DAX measures, a proper date table, and four report pages — built to connect to scheduled exports and refresh on its own.

What's next

Where this goes with a real client

The recommended next phase is full reporting automation: direct API connections to Shopify and the ad platforms, a scheduled weekly refresh, and a PDF summary delivered to your inbox every Monday morning — no manual exports, no spreadsheet drift.
